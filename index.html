Hello,I am Phillip Musyoka.

RESEARCH CONSULTANT: ECONOMIC RESEARCH.

I am a skilled quantitative and qualitative research analyst who provides evidence and draws lessons to inform decision-making processes for economic development and adaptive programming in economic development. My analysis skills are biased towards food security, nutrition and poverty, agricultural value chains, trade, resilience and markets, project evaluation and public policy. I have worked with diverse teams in several countries such as Kenya, Uganda, Tanzania, Rwanda, Burundi, South Sudan, Ethiopia, and Syria. I have consulted development agencies including the World Bank- & GFACE, MERCY CORPS, FAO-Kenya, FAO-Ethiopia, UN-Women, UN-WFP, Mercy Corps, TechnoServe, FARM AFRICA, CARE Int. at Turkey and South Sudan, EUCORD, AATF, Micro-Nutrient Initiative (MI), ChildFund, World Vision Kenya, Governments and Regional Economic blocks-EAC.

PROJECT IMPACT EVALUATION & QUANTIFICATION

FOOD SECURITY, NUTRITION AND POVERTY ANALYSIS
I am skilled in food security, nutrition and poverty analysis.. I am highly conversant with quantitative analysis of food security and nutrition indicators including- Food Consumption Scores (FCS), Household Hunger Scales (HHS), Household Dietary Diversity (HDDS), Coping Strategy Index (CSI) and Poverty incidence as well as poverty decomposition measures- Poverty incidence, gap and severity. I have analyzed food security and poverty in several assignments examining food security and poverty drivers to inform programmatic and public policy interventions.
